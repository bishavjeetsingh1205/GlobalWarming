INTRODUCTION :

What is Global Warming?

Earth is warming up, and humans are at least partially to blame. The causes, effects, and complexities of global warming are important to understand so that we can fight for the health of our planet. Global warming is the long-term warming of the planet overall temperature. Though this warming trend has been going on for a long time, its pace has significantly increased in the last hundred years. Global warming has presented another issue called climate change. Sometimes these phrases are used interchangeably, however, they are different. Climate change refers to changes in weather patterns and growing seasons around the world. It also refers to sea level rise caused by the expansion of warmer seas and melting ice sheets and glaciers which poses a serious threat to life on Earth

Why we need to act on Global Warming?

A warmer climate increases public health challenges like heat-aggravated illnesses, vector borne diseases, and decreased access to safe water and food. Delayed efforts to mitigate either carbon dioxide or Short-lived climate pollutants (SLCPs) - including black carbon, methane, hydrofluorocarbons, and tropospheric ozone emissions will have negative, and potentially irreversible, consequences for global warming, rising sea levels, food security, and public health. Due to relatively short lifetime of SLCPs in the atmosphere, ranging from a few days to a few decades, reducing their emissions can rapidly slow the rate of global temperature rise. Reducing SLCPs provides other significant benefits. These include preventing millions of premature deaths annually, improving food security by avoiding tens-of-millions of tonnes of annual staple crop losses, protecting vital ecosystems and ecosystem services, reducing the risk of dangerous and irreversible climate tipping points, and making significant contributions to achieving the Agenda 2030

CAUSES

Global warming is primarily caused by the presence of greenhouse gases in the Earth's atmosphere. The major contributors to this increase are human activities like burning of fossil fuels releasing significant amounts of carbon dioxide. Additionally, deforestation reduces the planet capacity to absorb carbon dioxide, while industrial processes and agriculture release other potent greenhouse gases. These activities amplify the natural greenhouse effect, leading to a rise in global temperatures and climate change.

EFFECTS

The effects of global warming are profound and widespread. Rising temperatures contribute to more frequent and severe weather events, such as hurricanes and droughts. Melting ice caps and glaciers lead to rising sea levels, posing a threat to coastal communities. Ecosystems are disrupted, and species are endangered as their habitats change. Additionally, global warming exacerbates health issues and food security challenges. Mitigating these effects requires urgent action to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and adapt to the changing climate.

SOLUTIONS

Global warming requires a multifaceted approach. Key solutions include transitioning to clean and renewable energy sources to reduce carbon emissions. Improving energy efficiency in transportation, buildings, and industries can also play a significant role. Reforestation and sustainable land management help capture carbon dioxide, while adopting sustainable agricultural practices reduces methane emissions. International cooperation and policies to limit emissions and promote climate resilience are essential for combatting global warming and mitigating its adverse effects on our planet.
